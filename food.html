EKPANG NKUKWO


Ekpang Nkukwo is a Nigerian native food common to the south south eastern parts of Nigeria, the Akwa Ibom and Cross river States of Nigeria. This special food is mostly prepared for special occasions such as traditional marriage.

Ekpang Nkukwo is rich in carbohydrate, protein, fat and oil, vitamins and minerals. Probably, why the process of cooking it consumes much time and energy (Joking).

Due to the long and tedious process of preparation, it is usually prepared with the help of other family members. Ekpang Nkukwo is best enjoyed hot and eaten with the hand. I wont forget to add in a cool environment (smile).


LIST OF INGREDIENTS

Please note that the quantity of ingredient vary with the number of people catered for.

Ingredients Measurements
Water yam (Dioscorea alata) 1 tuber
Cocoyam 7 pieces
Tender cocoyam leaves or pumpkin leaves (Ugwu leaves) 4 bunchs
Palm oil (red oil) 4 cooking spoons
Beef 1kg
fish (smoked or dried) 1 big size
Grounded cray fish/ shrimps 5 table spoon
Onions 2 big bulb
Shelled periwinkles 2 cup
Pepper 1 tablespoon
Scent leaves 1 bunch
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Seasoning (preferable: Knorr cubes) 4 cubes

PREPARATION

  1. Rinse and cook the beef with salt, pepper, onion and seasoning till done.
  2. Peel, wash and grate the cocoyam and water yam. Mix the two together and set aside.
  3. Rinse the tender cocoyam leaves and pumpkin leaves. Tear into medium pieces and set aside.
  4. Pour a generous amount of red palm oil insides the pot
  5. Slice some pumpkin leaves into the pot
  6. Scoop a small quantity of the grated mixed cocoyam and water yam into a piece of the torn cocoyam leaf or pumpkin leaf. Wrap with the leaf and place in the pot. Repeat this procedure till all the grated cocoyam (and water yam) is exhausted  .
  7. If you still have some left over of pumpkin (ugwu leaves) you can slice them and spread them on top of the wrapped cocoyam/wateryam.

COOKING PROCESS

  1. Boil some water in a kettle
  2. Pour the boiled water into the pot containing your wrapped cocoyam and water yam
  3. place the pot on the stove and allow to boil
  4. Add your already chopped meat and pieced fish with their stock into the pot
  5. Add your periwinkle
  6. Add your onions, grounded crayfish and swrimps, pepper and salt
  7. stir with a wooden spoons
  8. add seasoning
  9. Add the already chopped scent leaves
  10. Allow to smmimer for 5 mins
  11. Add palm oil (not compulsory)
  12. Allow to cook for 3 mins
  13. Remove from the stove and serve in a plate
